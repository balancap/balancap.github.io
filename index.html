Who I am?

I am a post-doc working in collaboration with professor Leonid Mytnik at the Israel Institute of Technology - Technion. My present work work mainly investigates the fractal geometry of continuous random trees and measure-valued processes such as stable super-Brownian motion. I am happily working on these subjects thanks to a joined fellowship of the Technion and the French Embassy in Israël.

From 2010 to 2013, I did my Ph.D. at the École Centrale Paris, MAS laboratory, under the supervision of professor Erick Herbin. My thesis was primarly focused on the use of (very!) analytical framework named 2-microlocal analysis to study and characterise sample path properties of stochastic processes. As the latter was obviously not cumbersome enough, I happened to mix with a bit of multifractal analysis to study nice classes of processes such linear fractional stable motion. For epsilon lovers and fans of fractional operators, you may find the full manuscript here.
